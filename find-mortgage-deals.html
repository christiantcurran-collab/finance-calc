Understanding Mortgage Rates

Fixed Rate

Your rate stays the same for the fixed period (typically 2, 3, 5, or 10 years). Protects you from rate rises but you won't benefit if rates fall.

Tracker

Follows the Bank of England base rate plus a set margin. Goes up and down with the base rate, offering flexibility but less certainty.

Variable Rate

Set by the lender and can change at any time. Usually the Standard Variable Rate (SVR) you move to after a fixed deal ends.

LTV Ratio

Loan-to-Value is the size of your mortgage as a percentage of the property value. Lower LTV = better rates. 60% LTV means a 40% deposit.

Product Fees

Upfront fees charged by lenders. Can often be added to the mortgage. Lower rate deals typically have higher fees.

APRC

Annual Percentage Rate of Charge includes the interest rate plus fees, giving a true cost comparison over the deal period.

Tips for Getting the Best Deal

Should I choose a 2-year or 5-year fix?

2-year fixes typically have lower rates but you'll need to remortgage sooner. 5-year fixes offer longer-term certainty and protection from rate rises. Consider your plans and risk tolerance.

How much deposit do I need?

Minimum is typically 5% (95% LTV) but you'll get much better rates with a larger deposit. Each 5% band (90%, 85%, 80%, etc.) usually unlocks better deals.

Should I pay the product fee upfront or add it to the mortgage?

Paying upfront is cheaper overall as you won't pay interest on it. But adding it to the mortgage spreads the cost and preserves your cash.

When should I start looking for a new deal?

Start looking 3-6 months before your current deal ends. You can usually reserve a rate up to 6 months in advance.

Do I need a mortgage broker?

Brokers can access exclusive deals and save you time. Many offer free services as they're paid commission by lenders. They're especially helpful for complex situations.
