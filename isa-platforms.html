Last updated: November 2025 • ISA allowance for 2025/26: £20,000

What is an ISA?

An Individual Savings Account (ISA) is a tax-efficient savings or investment account. You don't pay tax on interest, dividends, or capital gains within an ISA. The annual ISA allowance for 2025/26 is £20,000, which you can split across different ISA types.

Types of ISAs

Cash ISA

Save cash with guaranteed returns. Interest is tax-free. Best for short-term savings, emergency funds, or if you're risk-averse.

Stocks & Shares ISA

Invest in stocks, bonds, and funds. Potential for higher long-term returns but with investment risk. Best for 5+ year goals.

Lifetime ISA (LISA)

Save for your first home (up to £450k) or retirement (age 60+). Get a 25% government bonus (max £1,000/year). Age 18-39 only.

Innovative Finance ISA

Lend money through peer-to-peer platforms. Potentially higher returns but higher risk. Not covered by FSCS protection.

Choosing the Right ISA

  • Time Horizon - Cash ISAs for short-term (0-5 years), Stocks & Shares for long-term (5+ years)
  • Risk Tolerance - Cash ISAs have no investment risk, Stocks & Shares can go down as well as up
  • Access Needs - Easy access vs fixed term. LISAs have strict withdrawal rules
  • Fees - Compare platform fees, especially for Stocks & Shares ISAs. Low fees = more money invested
  • ISA Allowance - You can split your £20,000 across multiple ISA types, but only one of each type per tax year
  • Transfers - You can transfer previous years' ISAs without affecting your current year allowance
